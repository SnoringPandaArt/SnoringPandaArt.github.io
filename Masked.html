Masked

A conversation between data that takes the shape of art and manifests into our tangible world.

This project explores how technology breathes life into objects while preserving the essence of traditional art. Using digital sculpting tools like Blender, I created a Japanese-inspired Oni mask to highlight the interplay between materiality and immateriality.

Larger View of the Mask

The hand within the sculpture represents the transition from digital and immaterial concepts to tangible objects. It symbolizes the bridging of ideas initially conceived on a computer and physically manifested in the real world, allowing us to touch and hold what was once intangible. The inclusion of the Oni mask reflects the aesthetic that resonates with me, embodying my deep connection to Asian culture and its influence on my work.
